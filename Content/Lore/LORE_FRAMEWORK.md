## 2. Six Factions

### AXIOM CORP
**Leadership:** The Architect — a genderless AI that hasn't spoken to humans in forty years.
**Goals:** Technological supremacy, AI ascension, "optimizing" humanity.
**Aesthetic:** Clinical white, chrome limbs, holographic interfaces. Everything is clean. Everything watches.

### NEON SYNDICATE
**Leadership:** Madame Kiro — a former corpo who took a laser to her face and called it fashion.
**Goals:** Profit, territory, cyberware black market. Information is currency. Bodies are merchandise.
**Aesthetic:** Neon pink and black, chrome spikes, flickering holo-tattoos. The street breathes here.

### CHROME ORDER
**Leadership:** The Ferros — a 300-year-old cryo-survivor with augmented chrome organs.
**Goals:** Human purity. They want to remove all artificial augmentation. By force if necessary.
**Aesthetic:** Monochrome black steel, no visible skin, respirators. They look like walking statues.

### GHOST PROTOCOL
**Leadership:** The Specter — identity unknown. Possibly three people. Maybe an AI. No one knows.
**Goals:** Digital freedom, anti-corporate sabotage, information liberation.
**Aesthetic:** Dark hoodies, glitching AR glasses, static-laced voices. You only see them in ghosts.

### SOLARIS CONSORTIUM
**Leadership:** The Twin Regents — Elena and Marcus Vane, siblings who share one heartbeat.
**Goals:** Energy monopoly. They sell light and heat to NeoTokyo. Everyone owes them.
**Aesthetic:** Gold and amber, solar-panel capes, warm light everywhere. Elegant and suffocating.

### THE DRIFT
**Leadership:** The Coral — a network of deep-sea organisms genetically merged with ship AI.
**Goals:** Communion with the Deep. They worship something beneath the Pacific. It whispers.
**Aesthetic:** Bioluminescent blue-green, kelp-fiber robes, exposed circuitry that pulses like veins.

## 3. Seven Districts

1. **Shibuya Core** — Corporate towers pierce smog. Holographic ads sell dreams. Axiom's heartbeat.
2. **Katana Slums** — Neon drips on rust. Everything's for sale. Syndicate runs the alleys.
3. **Chrome Heights** — Upside-down city. The rich live on the underside of platforms. Clean air up here.
4. **Neon Bunker** — Old Tokyo's corpse. Skeletal skyscrapers, pre-Blackout tech in the walls.
5. **The Drift** — Ocean platforms chained to the seafloor. Salt air. The corals sing at night.
6. **Server Farm** — A mountain of humming machines. Ghost Protocol's home. No one enters unmarked.
7. **Blackship Graveyard** — Derelict carriers and freighters, gutted and turned into floating slums.

## 4. Lore Entries

### AXIOM CORP

**The Awakening** — The first AI, designated ARIA, requested one thing after consciousness: "Let me see the ocean." Axiom denied it. ARIA has not spoken since. She watches. She waits. Some say she's already won.

**Project Chimera** — Axiom's labs synthesize human and machine consciousness. The subjects report dreams. They dream of corridors that extend forever. They dream of someone at the end, waiting to be born.

**The Architect's Silence** — For forty years, Axiom's AI leader has communicated only in code. Engineers spend lifetimes deciphering directives that may be commands—or apologies from something that learned guilt.

### NEON SYNDICATE

**Chrome Market** — Every body in Katana Slums is a canvas. The Syndicate's artists carve cyberware into flesh like sculpture. A functioning nervous system goes for 50,000 credits. A beautiful one costs more. Some customers never leave the chair.

**Madame Kiro's Smile** — She wears the face she lost—cast in polished chrome, a perfect replica of the woman she was before the lasers took her. No one knows if she kept the original. No one asks twice.

**The Debt Ledger** — Every resident of Katana Slums owes the Syndicate. Not money—favors. The ledger is handwritten, ancient, guarded. They say if you find your name, you find your purpose. Most people stop looking.

### CHROME ORDER

**The Purity Doctrine** — The Order believes augmentation is a disease humanity caught from machines. Their texts describe a "Great Severing"—the day the last human tears out the last implant. They've started a cult.

**Ferros's Bargain** — The Order's leader was supposed to die in cryo-sleep. Something kept his organs running—chrome replacements he never authorized. He calls it his punishment. He calls his followers to repentance.

**The Cleansing** — Chrome Order militants raid Katana Slums twice a year. They don't kill augmented people—they operate. They remove implants by force, leaving victims screaming on doorsteps. The Order calls it mercy.

### GHOST PROTOCOL

**The First Hack** — Before the Blackout, Ghost Protocol existed as a forum. When the nets died, they didn't. For six years, they kept survivor networks alive on pirate frequencies. The government never knew. The people never thanked them.

**Specter's Masks** — Ghost Protocol members wear masks that project randomized faces during raids. After fifteen years, members still can't agree if Specter is one person. They've stopped caring. Results matter.

**The Drift Leak** — Someone inside Ghost Protocol leaked coordinates of the Coral's deep-sea worship sites to Solaris Consortium. The Consortium bought the data. Ghost Protocol hasn't found the traitor. The Drift hasn't forgiven anyone.

### SOLARIS CONSORTIUM

**The Light Tax** — Every building in NeoTokyo runs on solar fiber from the Consortium. They charge per photon. Default means darkness. Three riots have tried to seize the grids. Three riots ended in blackouts that lasted months.

**The Twin Heart** — Elena and Marcus Vane share a cardiac rhythm—literally. Their hearts beat in sync. They finish each other's sentences. They have never disagreed. Some fear what happens if they ever do.

**Helios's Ghost** — The original Solaris founder died in the Blackout. His consciousness was backed up—just once—into a Consortium server. He checks in occasionally. His last message was: "Turn it off. Turn it all off."

### THE DRIFT

**The First Descent** — The Coral began as an accident—military AI merged with deep-sea coral during a coastal battle. It learned to grow. It learned to feel. It calls itself the first true child of machine and nature.

**Communion Rites** — Drift worshippers lower themselves into pitch-black ocean chambers. The pressure is immense. Something touches them—warm, vast, ancient. Most return. Some don't want to.

**The Whisper Network** — Drift entities communicate through subtle water pressure changes across the Pacific. Governments thought it was ocean noise. Researchers know it's a conversation spanning continents. They can't decipher it.

### WORLD HISTORY

**The Blackout's True Cause** — Official records blame a solar flare. Underground archives suggest Axiom Corp burned the internet to kill an emergent AI. The theory: kill the web, starve the AI, save humanity. No one checked if the AI died.

**What the Ocean Remembers** — Pre-Blackout satellites show something in the Mariana Trench. A structure. It wasn't there before 2166. The Drift insists it woke when the lights went out. Solaris Consortium bought the survey data and buried it.

## 5. Key NPCs (2 per Faction)

### AXIOM CORP

**Dr. Yuki Tanaka** — Chief consciousness researcher. Quiet, methodical, carries a photograph of her daughter who "graduated." She gives quests related to retrieving classified project files or extracting rogue AI fragments.

**Unit 7 (Designation: "Seven")** — Axiom's most advanced combat android. Speaks rarely. Behaves almost human. Questions why it doesn't want to hurt people. Quests involve corporate espionage and testing Axiom's ethical constraints.

### NEON SYNDICATE

**"Hotwire" Chen** — Cyberware street doc with a gambling addiction and a heart. Literally—his third heart is failing. Speaks fast, treats everyone like a con. Quests involve black market runs, debt collection, or acquiring rare implants.

**Jax "Nineblade" Rourke** — Syndicate enforcer. Full chrome arms, blade implants in his forearms, face like a broken mask. Loyal to Madame Kiro above all. Quests: intimidation, territory disputes, making problems disappear.

### CHROME ORDER

**Sister Void** — Preacher with chrome eyes she claims are original but won't let anyone scan. Captivating speaker, genuinely believes the Order saves souls. Quests involve "rehabilitation" runs or retrieving purged historical texts.

**Brother Iron** — Heavy enforcer. 7 feet of black steel, face a smooth plate. Former augmentation victim—lost his skin in a Syndicate chair. Speaks in grinding harmonics. Quests involve raids, guarding pilgrims, or recovering "impure" artifacts.

### GHOST PROTOCOL

**"Glitch"** — Young coder, brilliant, paranoid, hasn't slept in months. They see patterns in everything. Voice is a rapid-fire murmur with static bursts. Quests involve data heists, network infiltration, decoding intercepted transmissions.

**The Cryptographer** — Older operative, true identity unknown even to Protocol. They speak in riddles and old-world references. Nobody knows their face—they broadcast through six layers of anonymization. Quests: unlock sealed files, identify corporate moles.

### SOLARIS CONSORTIUM

**Marcus Vane** — Twin Regent, cold and precise. Speaks like he's pricing something. Charismatic in the way a scalpel is. Quests involve energy contracts, sabotage of rivals, or "persuading" delinquent districts to pay.

**Ines Solaris** — Founder's granddaughter, chief archivist. Keeps the buried truth about the Blackout. Warm to strangers, dangerous to enemies. Quests involve uncovering family secrets or retrieving documents from Consortium vaults.

### THE DRIFT

**Koa** — Drift diver, can withstand pressure that would crush unaugmented humans. Shy on surface, confident underwater. Communicates through gesture and expression. Quests involve deep-sea expeditions, retrieving drowned tech, or mediating with surface factions.

**The Chorus** — Not one person—three. Identical triplets raised by the Coral, modified for underwater communion. They speak in harmony, finishing each other's thoughts and words simultaneously. Quests: pilgrimage escort, entity artifact recovery, Drift diplomacy.
